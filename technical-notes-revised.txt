# FVC Protocol Technical Notes - Revised
**Date:** August 6, 2025

## Blockchain & Network Choice

**Polygon Network Selection:**
- **EVM Compatibility:** Polygon's Ethereum Virtual Machine compatibility allows seamless deployment on both Polygon and Ethereum with minimal code changes, ensuring development efficiency and future flexibility.
- **Cost Efficiency:** Transaction fees of ~$0.01 enable micro-transactions and frequent user interactions without prohibitive costs.
- **Performance:** 65,000+ transactions per second capacity supports high-volume bonding and staking operations.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Polygon's infrastructure supports FCA-compliant KYC integration while maintaining user privacy through zero-knowledge proofs.

**Market Validation:** Polygon is used by major DeFi protocols including Aave, Uniswap, and Curve, demonstrating proven scalability and security for financial applications.

## Smart Contract Architecture

**Upgradeable Proxy Pattern:**
- **Technology:** OpenZeppelin's UUPS (Universal Upgradeable Proxy Standard) implementation
- **Benefits:** Enables contract upgrades without requiring users to interact with new addresses
- **Industry Standard:** Used by Compound, Aave, and other major DeFi protocols for governance flexibility
- **Security:** Maintains upgrade capability while preserving user funds and contract state

**Core Contract Suite:**
- **$FVCG.sol:** Fixed supply (1 billion) ERC-20 governance token with transfer restrictions and vesting schedules
- **Bonding.sol:** Multi-round discount bonding mechanism with configurable caps and vesting periods
- **Staking.sol:** Lockup-based staking with tiered APY rewards and early exit penalties
- **Compliance Modules:** Whitelisting and KYC integration for regulatory adherence

## Tokenomics Strategy

**Single Token Model (Revised):**
- **$FVCG:** Fixed 1 billion supply, distributed through bonding, staking rewards, and governance participation
- **Distribution:** 40% bonding rounds, 30% staking rewards, 20% governance incentives, 10% treasury reserves
- **Vesting:** Linear vesting over 3-12 months for bonding participants and governance participants

**Market Positioning:** Single token model simplifies user experience and reduces complexity compared to dual-token systems used by protocols like Olympus DAO.

## Bonding & Treasury Mechanism

**Discount-Based Bonding:**
- **Initial Discount:** 20% declining linearly over 30 days to 10%
- **Accepted Assets:** FCA-approved stablecoins (USDC, DAI, USDT)
- **Vesting Period:** 3-month linear vesting with early exit penalties
- **Caps:** 10M USDC per round, 1M USDC per wallet to prevent concentration

**Treasury Management:**
- **Multi-sig Security:** Gnosis Safe implementation with 3-of-5 signature requirement for treasury operations
- **Institutional Migration:** Planned migration to institutional custody solutions (e.g., Credit Suisse) for enhanced security and compliance
- **Fund Allocation:** 60% startup funding, 30% staking rewards, 10% operational reserves
- **Industry Comparison:** Similar to Olympus DAO's treasury diversification but focused on SME funding

## Staking & Rewards System

**Tiered Staking Structure:**
- **Lockup Periods:** 3, 6, and 12-month options
- **APY Ranges:** 5-10% based on lockup duration and protocol performance
- **Reward Distribution:** Protocol revenue sharing plus $FVCG token rewards
- **Early Exit Penalties:** 10-25% penalty based on remaining lockup time

**Market Validation:** Similar to Aave's staking model but with SME-focused revenue generation.

## Compliance & Governance Framework

**KYC Integration:**
- **Technology:** Sumsub selected as primary KYC solution, chosen by major crypto exchanges including Binance, Kraken, and Coinbase
- **Compliance:** FCA regulatory requirements for UK startup funding
- **Privacy:** Zero-knowledge proof implementation to protect user data
- **Access Control:** Whitelist-based participation in bonding and governance
- **Market Validation:** Sumsub's crypto-native approach and proven track record with regulatory compliance

**Governance Structure:**
- **Quadratic Voting:** Reduces whale influence and promotes democratic participation
- **Proposal Types:** Funding allocations, protocol parameters, treasury management
- **Voting Periods:** 7-day proposal periods with 3-day execution delays
